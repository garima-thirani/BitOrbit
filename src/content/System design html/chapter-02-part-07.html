The Global Pizza Chain

Imagine a pizza chain with only one kitchen in New York. A customer in Tokyo orders a pizza. By the time it arrives, it's cold and stale. The solution? Open kitchens in every city. Now every customer gets a fresh pizza in minutes. Software works the same way. A single data center serving global users means high latency for everyone far away.

Geographic Distribution deploys application instances in multiple regions worldwide to reduce latency, improve availability, and provide disaster recovery.
🌍 Why Deploy Globally?
⚡ Reduce Latency
Serve users from the nearest region. Round-trip times drop from 300ms to 10ms.
🛡 Disaster Recovery
If one data center fails, traffic is routed to another region.
📈 Scale Beyond Limits
Single region has capacity limits. Global deployment allows infinite scaling.
🔒 Compliance
Some regulations require data to stay within specific geographic boundaries.
🏛 Active-Active vs Active-Passive

🟢 Active-Active

All regions serve traffic simultaneously. Provides the best latency and availability. Requires sophisticated data replication and conflict resolution. Used by Netflix, Google.

🟡 Active-Passive

One region serves traffic. The second region is on standby. On failure, traffic fails over. Simpler but wastes resources. Used by many enterprise applications.
